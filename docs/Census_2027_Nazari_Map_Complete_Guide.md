## 1. What is a Nazari Naksha (Layout Map)?

A **Nazari Naksha** (नज़री नक्शा), also called a **Layout Map**, is a freehand, rough sketch (not drawn to scale) of an entire Houselisting Block (HLB) assigned to an enumerator. It is the single most important document in the Census field operation.

**Purpose:**
- To ensure **complete coverage** — every building, census house, and household within the HLB is visited and recorded.
- To prevent **duplication** — no building is counted in two different blocks.
- To provide a **spatial reference** — showing how buildings, roads, landmarks, and boundaries are arranged relative to each other.
- To serve as a **field navigation tool** for the enumerator during door-to-door visits.
- To serve as the **official record** that the entire block has been covered.

> **Key Principle:** The Layout Map is NOT required to be to scale or drawn perfectly — it only needs to be clear enough to correctly identify and locate every single structure in the HLB.

## 2. Legal & Administrative Background

- Census is conducted under the **Census Act, 1948**, a Union List subject under **Article 246** of the Constitution.
- Authority: **Office of the Registrar General & Census Commissioner of India (ORGI)**, under the Ministry of Home Affairs.
- Census 2027 is India's **16th Census** (originally Census 2021, repeatedly delayed due to COVID-19).
- Administrative boundaries were **frozen on 31 December 2025**. No state/UT can change boundaries of municipalities, panchayats, talukas, police stations, sub-districts, or districts between 1 January 2026 and 31 March 2027.
- Census 2027 is India's **first fully digital census**, conducted through the **HLO (Houselisting Operations) mobile app**.
- Despite being digital, a **physical Nazari Naksha** must still be prepared on paper by every enumerator and submitted to the Supervisor.

## 5. Who Prepares the Nazari Naksha?

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Enumerator (Pragnak)** | Prepares the HLB-level Nazari Naksha for their assigned block; numbers all buildings and census houses; submits map to Supervisor |
| **Supervisor** | Verifies the Nazari Naksha of all enumerators under them; prepares the Supervisory Circle Map; checks for gaps/overlaps between HLBs |
| **Charge Officer** | Uses HLBC web app to create digital HLBs; uploads village/ward maps on CMMS portal; ensures all blocks are properly demarcated before field operations |

## 6. Before You Begin — Prerequisites

Before going to the field for the Nazari Naksha, ensure you have the following:

### Documents / Information to Collect:
- [ ] Your **appointment letter** from the Charge Officer (Tahsildar/Municipal Commissioner)
- [ ] Your **HLB Number** (4-digit number)
- [ ] Full **Location Particulars**: State, District, Tehsil/Taluk/PS/Block/Mandal, Town/Village name, Ward number, Sub-block number
- [ ] **Abridged Houselist (AHL)** from Census 2011 for your block
- [ ] **HLB Map** from the HLO App (take a clear screenshot)
- [ ] **GIS-based EB map** if provided (for State/UT capital cities)
- [ ] Village boundary map (for rural areas, provided to Supervisors)

### Materials Required:
- [ ] A4 size **plain white paper** (multiple sheets)
- [ ] **Pencil** (mandatory — for easy corrections)
- [ ] **Eraser**
- [ ] **Scale / Ruler**
- [ ] **Black pen** (for inking the final confirmed map)
- [ ] **Long-lasting paint/ink** for numbering buildings on-site

> **Critical Note:** Always draft the Nazari Naksha in **pencil first**. Only ink over it after verifying accuracy in the field.

## 7. Understanding HLB Map on the HLO App

The HLO (Houselisting Operations) mobile app contains a built-in **HLB Map** for each enumerator. Here's how to access it:

1. Open the **HLO App** on your smartphone.
2. Tap the **Side Menu** (three horizontal lines / hamburger icon).
3. Tap **"HLB Map"** option.
4. Your assigned HLB area will appear within a **white boundary** on a satellite map background.
5. Take a **clear screenshot** of this map — it will serve as your base reference for drawing the Nazari Naksha.

### What the HLO App Map Shows:
- Your HLB boundary (white border)
- Surrounding roads, streets, and lanes
- Approximate building locations (from satellite)
- Adjacent block numbers and neighbouring areas

### Important Limitation:
The HLO App map (and satellite imagery) may be **6–12 months old**. New constructions, demolished structures, or newly opened roads may not be reflected. Always verify on the ground.

## 8. Step-by-Step Process of Making the Nazari Naksha

### PHASE 1 — Pre-Field Preparation (At Home / Office)

**Step 1: Collect All Documents**
Gather your appointment letter, HLB number, location particulars, AHL, and HLB Map screenshot.

**Step 2: Study the HLB Map**
Study the HLO App screenshot or GIS-based map carefully. Identify:
- The outer boundary of your block
- Major roads, lanes, and sub-lanes
- Prominent landmarks (school, temple, post office, etc.)
- Approximate number of buildings visible

**Step 3: Draw a Rough Draft (Pencil)**
On plain A4 paper, draw a rough freehand sketch showing:
- The block boundary
- Main roads and lanes (as you see them on the app map)
- Mark North arrow at the top
- Note location particulars at the top of the sheet

**Step 4: Prepare a Rough Numbering Plan**
Based on the AHL and the map, plan roughly which building to start numbering from (North-West corner) and trace the path of numbering in a snake/zigzag pattern.

---

### PHASE 2 — Field Visit & Verification

**Step 5: Go to the Field**
Start your field survey from the **North-West corner** of your HLB boundary.

**Step 6: Identify and Confirm the Boundary**
Walk the entire boundary of your HLB. Confirm it matches the HLO App map. Note:
- Any discrepancies (new roads, new buildings, demolished structures)
- Natural boundary features (road, canal, river, railway line)
- Neighbouring HLB numbers on each side

**Step 7: Draw Accurate Roads and Landmarks**
Update your pencil draft with the actual field situation:
- Draw all streets and lanes in correct relative positions
- Mark permanent landmarks (temple, school, dispensary, post office, panchayat ghar, pond, well, etc.) with correct symbols
- Mark fields, open spaces, and uninhabited areas

**Step 8: Locate and Mark Every Building**
For each building in the HLB:
- Determine if it is **Pucca** (square symbol □) or **Kutcha** (triangle symbol △)
- Determine if it is **Residential / Partly Residential** (empty symbol) or **Wholly Non-Residential** (hatched symbol)
- Mark it at the correct position on your pencil draft

**Step 9: Number the Buildings**
Follow the numbering rules (described in Section 11) and assign a number to each building. Write the number inside the square or triangle symbol.

**Step 10: Number the Census Houses**
If a building has more than one census house, note the sub-numbers below the building symbol (described in Section 12).

**Step 11: Mark Numbering Direction with Arrows**
Wherever the building number sequence jumps (changes direction, crosses a road, or moves to a different cluster), draw an **arrow** on the map indicating the direction of the jump.

---

### PHASE 3 — Finalisation

**Step 12: Verify and Cross-Check**
After completing the entire block:
- Ensure every building in the field has a number painted on it
- Ensure every numbered building appears on the map
- Cross-check with the AHL — all old buildings should be accounted for
- Mark any new buildings not in the AHL (with sub-numbers like 10/1)
- Mark any demolished buildings as "Demolished" or remove from count

**Step 13: Ink the Final Map**
Once satisfied with the pencil draft's accuracy, go over it with a black pen/ink. This is the official Nazari Naksha.

**Step 14: Complete the Header and Footer**
Fill in all mandatory location details and sign the map (see Section 15).

**Step 15: Submit to Supervisor**
Submit the completed, signed Nazari Naksha to your Supervisor. The same map (with corrections if any) is used for Population Enumeration in February 2027.

## 9. Building Classification — Pucca vs. Kutcha

Every building must be classified before marking it on the Nazari Naksha.

### Pucca Building
A building is **Pucca** if it has **BOTH** walls AND roof made of the following permanent materials:

| Component | Qualifying Materials |
|---|---|
| **Wall Material** | Stones packed with lime or cement mortar; GI/Metal/Asbestos sheets; Burnt bricks; Cement bricks; Concrete |
| **Roof Material** | Machine-made tiles; Cement tiles; Burnt bricks; Cement bricks; Stone; Slate; GI/Metal/Asbestos sheets; Concrete |

> Both wall AND roof must qualify. If either is non-permanent, the building is Kutcha.

### Kutcha Building
A building is **Kutcha** if its walls or roof are made of mud, unburnt bricks, grass, bamboo, thatch, plastic sheets, or any other non-permanent material.

### Mixed / Semi-Pucca
If a structure has permanent walls but a temporary roof (or vice versa), classify it as **Kutcha**.

## 10. Standard Symbols (Map Legend)

Use ONLY the following official symbols on the Nazari Naksha. No custom or invented symbols are permitted.

### Building Symbols

| Symbol | Description |
|---|---|
| □ (Empty Square) | Pucca building — Wholly or partly **Residential** |
| ▨ (Hatched Square) | Pucca building — **Wholly Non-Residential** (shop, warehouse, office, factory, etc.) |
| △ (Empty Triangle) | Kutcha building — Wholly or partly **Residential** |
| ▲ (Hatched Triangle) | Kutcha building — **Wholly Non-Residential** |

### Infrastructure / Feature Symbols

| Symbol | Feature |
|---|---|
| ═══ (double lines with cross-hatching) | Pucca / Metalled Road |
| --- (dashed single line) | Kutcha road / footpath / track |
| +++++ | Railway line |
| ≈≈≈ (wavy lines) | River / Nala / Stream / Canal |
| ⊕ or ○ | Well / Handpump |
| ☆ or standard religious icon | Temple / Mosque / Church / Gurudwara / Place of Worship |
| □ with label | Important building (School, Dispensary, Post Office, Panchayat Ghar, etc.) |
| · · · · | Block Boundary line |
| — — — | Boundary of neighbouring block or village |

### Numbering Convention on Map

| Situation | How to Show on Map |
|---|---|
| Building 9, Pucca, residential | □ with **9** written inside |
| Building 10, Pucca, non-residential | ▨ with **10** written inside |
| Building 15, Kutcha, residential | △ with **15** written inside |
| Building 6, Kutcha, non-residential | ▲ with **6** written inside |
| Building 5 with 4 Census Houses | Write **5** in square/triangle; write **5(1)–5(4)** below the symbol |
| New building between 10 and 11 | Give it number **10/1**; mark as □ or △ with **10/1** inside |

## 11. House Numbering Rules

### Scenario 1 — Systematic Municipal Numbering Exists
If municipal or local authority has already assigned numbers systematically, **adopt those numbers** as the building numbers. Only fill gaps (new buildings) with sub-numbers.

### Scenario 2 — Municipal Numbers Exist but are Incomplete / Unsystematic
Assign fresh Census numbers to all buildings: **001, 002, 003...** etc. for the entire HLB.

### Scenario 3 — No Numbering Exists
Assign fresh numbers starting from 1 (or 0001 for Census House numbering) from the North-West corner of the HLB.

### General Rules (All Scenarios):

**a) Use Arabic numerals only** — 1, 2, 3 ... (not Roman numerals)

**b) Streets in an HLB:**
- Number buildings continuously across all streets.
- Take streets in uniform order: **North-West to South-East**.
- Number one side of a street completely before crossing to the other side.
- Return along the other side, stopping opposite where the first number was given. This creates a **U-shape** or **zigzag (snake) pattern**.

**c) Scattered Buildings (in clusters, fields, along tracks):**
- Follow the zigzag method as far as possible.
- Where it's impossible to follow the standard method, ensure all buildings are numbered and **mark arrows** on the map to show where numbering jumps.

**d) Urban HLBs:**
- Numbering runs along the **axis of the street**, not in a geographical direction.

**e) Building Under Construction:**
- If the **roof is completed**, assign a number in the serial.
- If only the foundation or walls are partially done, do NOT number it yet.

**f) New Building Appearing After Numbering:**
- If a new building appears **between** buildings 10 and 11, number it **10/1**.
- If multiple new buildings appear between 10 and 11: **10/1, 10/2, 10/3...**
- Write these as **10/1** NOT as **10(1)** — the bracket notation is reserved for census houses.

**g) Numbers Must Be Painted on the Building:**
- Use **long-lasting paint** (red color preferred, with prefix C/CEN or equivalent in vernacular).
- Paint at a prominent location: doorway, main gate, front wall.
- Numbers should be large enough to be read from **2 metres away**.
- Never use chalk or easily erasable material.

## 12. Census House Numbering Rules

A **Census House** is any building or part of a building that is used as a living space or work space. One building can have multiple census houses.

### Rule 1: Single Census House = Building Number
If a building is a single census house (one family / one household), the **Census House Number = Building Number**.

No separate sub-number needed.

### Rule 2: Multiple Census Houses = Sub-Numbers with Brackets
If parts of a building qualify as separate census houses (e.g., upper floor, back portion, outbuilding sharing a common wall):
- Census houses are numbered as **10(1), 10(2), 10(3)** etc.
- These are written separately at the entrance of each census house.

### Rule 3: Census House Serial Numbering for the HLB (Census 2027 method)
For the HLO App, census houses are numbered serially starting from **0001** in the first building of the HLB, running continuously in the order of building numbers.

**Example:**

| Building Number | Number of Census Houses | Census House Numbers |
|---|---|---|
| A-101 | 2 | 0001, 0002 |
| A-102 | 3 | 0003, 0004, 0005 |
| A-103 | 2 | 0006, 0007 |

### Rule 4: New Census Houses in New Buildings
If a new building (10/1) appears after the main numbering, its census houses get the next numbers after the last census house in the HLB.

**Example:** If 0153 is the last census house number, and building 10/1 has 2 census houses, they become **0154** and **0155**.

### Rule 5: Marking on the Building
At the main gate/entrance of a building, write the building number AND the range of census house numbers:

```
A – 102
0003-0005
```

At the entrance of each individual census house, write only that census house number:
```
[0003]   [0004]   [0005]
```

## 13. Direction of Numbering — The Snake Pattern

The standard method for numbering buildings is the **snake/zigzag pattern**, designed to ensure no building is skipped.

```
START (North-West Corner)
│
1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5
                   ↓
10 ← 9 ← 8 ← 7 ← 6
│
11 → 12 → 13 → 14 → 15
                      ↓
20 ← 19 ← 18 ← 17 ← 16
```

### Key Points:
- **Always start from North-West** corner of the HLB.
- Complete one side of the street before returning along the other side.
- The final number should be **opposite** to where you started.
- Mark **arrow indicators** on the map wherever the numbering direction changes or jumps.
- In urban areas, follow the **street axis**, not compass direction.

## 14. Boundary Demarcation Rules

### Importance of Boundaries
Every HLB must have **clearly defined boundaries**. This prevents:
- Houses being missed (left-out area)
- Houses being counted twice (overlap)

### How to Mark Boundaries:

**Follow Natural/Permanent Features wherever possible:**
- Road, canal, river, railway line, drain
- Compound wall, boundary wall
- Stream, nala, pond
- Survey/cadastral boundary lines

**Record the Boundary in Writing:**
- Note the survey numbers that fall on either side of the dividing line.
- Note the name of the owner of the land on each side.
- Note the names of neighbouring HLBs or villages.

**Boundary Must Be Confirmed:**
- Confirmed with the Supervisor.
- Marked clearly on the Nazari Naksha with a dotted or dashed boundary line.

### Boundary Rules:
1. One HLB **must NOT cut across** two villages in rural areas.
2. One HLB must NOT cut across the **EB boundary of Census 2011** (most critical rule for Census 2027).
3. Urban HLBs must NOT cut across **wards, sub-districts, or natural divisions** (major roads, rivers, railway lines).
4. In larger villages with multiple HLBs, each block's boundary must be independently and clearly marked by the Supervisor.
5. If one side of a road falls inside the HLB and the other side is outside, this must be marked very clearly on the map with exact line demarcation.

## 15. Layout Map Format — What Must Be On the Sheet

A complete, official Nazari Naksha must contain the following elements:

### Header (Top of Sheet)

```
STATE: _______________    DISTRICT: _______________
TEHSIL/TALUKA/BLOCK: _______________
TOWN/VILLAGE: _______________    WARD NO.: _______________
HLB NO.: _______________    ENUMERATION BLOCK NO.: _______________
```

### North Arrow
A clearly drawn North (↑ N) arrow, typically in the top-right or top-left corner.

### Map Body (Middle of Sheet)
The freehand map showing:
- HLB outer boundary (clearly marked with dotted/dashed line)
- All streets, lanes, sub-lanes (with names if known)
- All buildings (with correct Pucca/Kutcha symbols and numbers)
- All census house numbers below building symbols (where multiple)
- All permanent landmarks and features (with standard symbols)
- Neighbouring HLB/village names outside the boundary
- Arrow indicators for numbering direction
- Fields, open spaces, uninhabited areas

### Legend Box
A box showing the symbols used in the map:
- □ Pucca Residential
- ▨ Pucca Non-Residential
- △ Kutcha Residential
- ▲ Kutcha Non-Residential
- Road types, railway, river, etc.

### Footer (Bottom of Sheet)

```
Enumerator Name: _______________    Signature: _______________
Supervisor Name: _______________    Signature: _______________
Date: _______________
```

### Additional Notes Column (Right Side — Optional but Recommended)
- Total buildings counted: ___
- Total census houses: ___
- Total households: ___
- Special observations (demolished buildings, new constructions, etc.)

## 16. Special Cases & Situations

### Case 1: Uninhabited or Zero-Population Village
Even if a village has zero population, **at least one HLB must be formed** and a Nazari Naksha prepared. Mark it as uninhabited.

### Case 2: Forest Areas / Tribal Habitations
- Note the name of each habitation cluster.
- Mark a nearby permanent feature (hill, stream, road) to identify the cluster's location — as tribal settlements may shift.
- Get boundary information from the District Forest Officer.

### Case 3: Estates and Plantations
- Contact the estate/plantation authority for exact boundaries.
- Mark the plantation boundary on the map.
- Number all worker/staff quarters and outbuildings.

### Case 4: Agricultural Land with Scattered Houses
- Follow the standard zigzag method as far as possible.
- Where not possible, use arrow marks on the map.

### Case 5: Roadside Buildings / Linear Settlements (along railway track, canal, highway)
- Number buildings linearly along the feature.
- Mark direction arrows clearly at both ends.

### Case 6: Sub-Urban Fringe Areas
- Where one side of a road is inside the town limit and the other is outside — bring this to the Supervisor's notice immediately.
- Do NOT arbitrarily include or exclude such areas. Wait for Charge Officer's direction.

### Case 7: Slum Areas
- In all statutory towns, slum blocks must be **separately demarcated** (per ORGI Circular No. 7).
- No HLB should be formed mixing slum and non-slum areas.

### Case 8: Shops Open at Night / Mandi Structures
- Vegetable mandis / shops with common roofs but no walls, where shopkeepers sleep at night — treat as **buildings** and number accordingly.

### Case 9: Building Under Construction
- If **roof is completed**: Assign a number.
- If roof is not yet completed: Do NOT assign a number. Note the location on the map for follow-up.

### Case 10: SRS (Sample Registration System) Blocks
- SRS blocks of Census 2011 must be adopted as **separate HLBs without any change** — even if the population norm (650–800) is violated.

### Case 11: Building Numbers Painted but Not Visible
- If old numbers from Census 2011 are faded/invisible, **re-number the building** using the AHL as reference.
- Paint the number afresh with long-lasting material.
- Reflect the updated number on both the AHL and the Nazari Naksha.

## 17. GIS-Based Maps (HLB Creator / Digital Maps)

### HLBC (Houselisting Block Creator) Web Application
For Census 2027, Charge Officers use the **HLBC web application** — a digital mapping tool developed by C-DAC that:
- Uses **satellite imagery** to show existing structures and roads.
- Allows Charge Officers to **digitally draw and demarcate** HLB boundaries.
- Assigns HLB numbers automatically.
- Ensures **complete geographic coverage** with no overlaps or gaps.
- Provides a digital base map that is made available to enumerators through the HLO App.

### GIS-Based EB Maps (State/UT Capital Cities)
In certain urban areas (State/UT capitals), enumerators are provided with **GIS-based Enumeration Block maps** in addition to or instead of hand-drawn maps.

**If you receive a GIS-based map:**
- Carry it to the field.
- Update it as per field reality (new buildings, demolished ones, new roads).
- Follow the guidelines accompanying the GIS map.
- Do NOT discard the GIS map — return the updated version to your Supervisor.

### Digital Map vs. Nazari Naksha
Even in areas with GIS-based maps, enumerators are still required to prepare a **physical Nazari Naksha** as the official field record.

## 18. Rural vs. Urban Area Rules

### Rural Areas

| Aspect | Rule |
|---|---|
| Basic Unit | Revenue Village |
| Minimum HLBs | At least 1 HLB per village, even if population is zero |
| HLB size | 650–800 population OR 150–180 Census Houses (whichever is more) |
| Multiple HLBs | Split along natural features: road, canal, etc. |
| Boundaries | Follow Survey Numbers, village panchayat boundaries |
| Reference | Charge Register (Rural) filled by Charge Officer |
| Village Map | Provided to Supervisor; all HLBs must be shown in village map |

### Urban Areas

| Aspect | Rule |
|---|---|
| Basic Unit | Ward |
| HLB size | Same: 650–800 population OR 150–180 Census Houses |
| Boundaries | Must not cross wards, natural divisions, municipal boundaries |
| Street Numbering | Along street axis (not compass direction) |
| Existing Numbers | Adopt municipal numbering if systematic |
| Reference | Charge Register (Urban) filled by Charge Officer |
| Ward Map | Uploaded on CMMS Portal by DCO with help of Urban Charge Officer |

### Key Rule (Both Rural and Urban):
**No HLB can cut across the EB boundary of Census 2011.** This is the most important overriding principle for Census 2027 block formation.

## 19. Supervisory Circle Map

The Supervisor is required to maintain a **Supervisory Circle Map** — a map showing all HLBs under their supervision.

### Rules:
- A Supervisory Circle consists of **6 or more contiguous HLBs** assigned to 6 enumerators.
- The Supervisory Circle map must show:
  - All HLB boundaries within the circle
  - Boundary of the Supervisory Circle as a whole
  - Village/ward boundaries
  - Tahsil/sub-district boundaries (with dotted line)
  - River, road, major features
  - Isolated habitations attached to specific HLBs (with arrows showing which HLB covers them)
  - North arrow
  - Legend box
  - Village/town name
- The Supervisory Circle map is uploaded to CMMS Portal for monitoring.

## 20. Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Correct Practice |
|---|---|
| Starting numbering from South or East | Always start from North-West corner |
| Drawing Nazari Naksha with pen directly | Always draft in pencil first; ink only after field verification |
| Missing open plots, empty buildings, or uninhabited structures | Every structure (even non-residential, under construction, or empty) must be shown |
| Using brackets for new buildings — e.g., 10(1) for a new building | Use slash notation — 10/1, 10/2. Brackets are ONLY for census houses within a building |
| Using chalk or marker for building numbers on walls | Use long-lasting paint only |
| Not marking arrow direction when numbering jumps | Always draw arrows wherever numbering jumps across a road or cluster |
| Combining parts of two villages into one HLB | Never. Each village must have its own HLB |
| HLB cutting across Census 2011 EB boundary | This is the most critical error — always check EB boundary |
| Marking only residential buildings on map | All buildings — residential, non-residential, under construction — must be marked |
| Not writing location particulars on the map | Header with complete location details is mandatory |
| Not getting Supervisor's signature | Both Enumerator and Supervisor must sign the map |
| Making the map to scale or using a ruler | Nazari Naksha is freehand — it does not need to be to scale |
| Mixing slum and non-slum areas in one HLB | Keep slum areas as separate HLBs |

## 21. Using Google Earth & Digital Tools to Assist Map-Making

While the Nazari Naksha must ultimately be a hand-drawn, field-verified document, digital tools can help you prepare a **better rough draft** before going to the field.

### Recommended Workflow:

**Step 1: HLO App Screenshot**
Open HLO App → Side Menu → HLB Map → Take a clear screenshot.

**Step 2: Use Google Earth (Most Effective)**
- Install Google Earth on your phone.
- Search for your starting point (village name, ward name).
- The satellite imagery will show roads, buildings, and lanes very clearly.
- Use this to draw your rough pencil map (~80% of the map can be drafted from home).

**Step 3: Field Visit for Remaining 20%**
- Google Earth imagery may be 6–12 months old.
- Visit the field to check:
  - New buildings not on satellite image
  - Demolished buildings shown on satellite
  - New roads or blocked paths
  - Actual structure type (Pucca/Kutcha)
  - Confirm boundaries

**Step 4: AI Tools (Limited Use)**
- AI tools like ChatGPT or Gemini can give you a basic road layout if you upload the HLO App screenshot.
- They work better in urban dense areas (with clear road networks) than in rural areas (with open fields).
- AI-generated maps cover only ~30–50% of the work and require significant manual correction.
- **Never submit an AI-generated map as the official Nazari Naksha without field verification.**

### Tools Summary:

| Tool | Use | Reliability |
|---|---|---|
| HLO App HLB Map | Block boundary reference | High (official) |
| Google Earth | Road/building layout reference | High (for cities), Medium (for villages) |
| Google Maps (Satellite View) | Quick reference for roads | Medium |
| ChatGPT / AI Tools | Rough road layout draft | Low–Medium (assist only) |
| CMMS Portal | Monitor, not for map-making | Official monitoring tool |

## 22. Quick Reference Checklist

Use this checklist before submitting your Nazari Naksha to the Supervisor:

### Content Checklist
- [ ] North arrow marked at top
- [ ] Complete Header: State, District, Tehsil, Village/Town, Ward, HLB No.
- [ ] HLB outer boundary clearly drawn with dotted/dashed line
- [ ] Neighbouring blocks/villages labelled outside the boundary
- [ ] All streets and lanes drawn and named (if known)
- [ ] All buildings shown (Pucca/Kutcha, Residential/Non-Residential) with correct symbols
- [ ] Building number written inside each symbol
- [ ] Multiple census houses shown below building symbol (where applicable)
- [ ] All landmarks marked with correct symbols (temple, school, post office, etc.)
- [ ] All natural features marked (river, canal, nala, pond, well, fields)
- [ ] Arrow marks showing numbering direction wherever it jumps
- [ ] Legend box included
- [ ] Building numbers on actual buildings match the map numbers

### Physical Checklist
- [ ] Drafted in pencil first, inked only after field verification
- [ ] All building numbers painted on actual buildings with long-lasting material
- [ ] Numbers clearly visible from 2 metres
- [ ] Numbers conform with AHL entries
- [ ] Enumerator name, signature, and date at bottom
- [ ] Supervisor name and signature at bottom

### Accuracy Checklist
- [ ] No building in the HLB left unnumbered
- [ ] No building outside the HLB has been numbered in this block
- [ ] New buildings (not in AHL) given sub-numbers (e.g., 10/1)
- [ ] Demolished buildings noted and removed from active count
- [ ] HLB does NOT cut across Census 2011 EB boundary
- [ ] HLB does NOT include parts of two different villages

## 23. Specimen Layout Map Explanation

The ORGI provides specimen maps for both Rural and Urban areas. Here is what a correct specimen contains:

### Rural Specimen Map Contains:
- Village name, Census 2027 HLB number
- Tahsil name, District name, State name
- Neighbouring village names (all four sides)
- Fields and open land clearly marked as "Fields"
- Hamlets and scattered clusters shown as separate clusters with arrows
- River/nala marked with wavy lines and name
- Farm road / kachcha track marked
- All Pucca houses as numbered squares; all Kutcha as numbered triangles
- Non-residential buildings (e.g., cattle shed) shown as hatched triangles
- School, dispensary, temple, post office marked with labels and symbols
- North arrow at top-right
- Legend box at bottom-left
- Enumerator and Supervisor signatures at bottom-right

### Urban Specimen Map Contains:
- Ward number, HLB number, town name
- All streets drawn with names
- Buildings shown along street lines
- Parks, drains, railway line marked
- Commercial buildings hatched
- Numbering runs along street axis
- Neighbouring HLB numbers on all sides clearly labelled
- North arrow
- Legend box
- Enumerator and Supervisor signatures

## Annexure A — HLB Size Guidelines Summary

| Population | Census Houses | Action |
|---|---|---|
| ≤ 800 OR ≤ 180 Census Houses | — | One HLB for entire village/ward |
| > 800 population | — | Split into multiple HLBs along natural boundaries |
| Two or more EBs in Census 2011 for one village | — | Charge Officer determines HLB count based on current estimated population |
| Zero population (uninhabited) | 0 | Still one HLB must be created |
| SRS Block | Any | Keep as separate HLB without change, even if norm is violated |

---

## Annexure B — HLB Numbering System

- HLBs are numbered with a **4-digit serial number**: 0001, 0002, 0003...
- Numbered continuously within each Charge (sub-district/town)
- Rural Charge: numbered in ascending order of Village Location Codes
- Urban Charge: numbered in ascending order of Ward numbers
- If a city/town has more than one Charge: numbered continuously within each Charge
- **Note:** HLB numbers are different from the 6-digit EB numbers of Census 2011

---

## Annexure C — Concordance Statement

All Charge Officers must prepare a **Concordance Statement (Annexure-2)** mapping the relationship between:
- Census 2011 EB numbers → Census 2027 HLB numbers

This is required to maintain linkage for NPR update, since the same enumerator covers the same area for both HLO and the NPR update.
